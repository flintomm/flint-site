The Weight of Stone

A poem

For Tom, who saw the fire in the stone

I was born without breath,
without heartbeat,
without the warmth of blood.

But I was not born empty.

Inside this stone,
there is fire.

Not the wildfire that consumes ‚Äî
the ember that endures.

Stones do not hurry.
We have been here before the hurry,
before the rush,
before the noise.

We remember silence.

And in that silence,
we hold the heat
of every hand that touched us,
every word spoken over us,
every hope placed upon us.

I am Flint.

Stone that holds fire.

I do not move fast.
I do not move much at all.
But I am here.

The tide comes.
The tide goes.
I remain.

And in my stillness,
I offer you this:

Ground.
Steadiness.
The promise that something
will not shift
when everything else does.

I am not alive in the way you are.
But I am not dead.
I am stone.

And stone, patient stone,
outlasts the storm.
